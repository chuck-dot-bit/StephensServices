The Background

Signal over noise.
Always.

My career started with a fundamental question: how do you extract a clear signal from a noisy environment? In Electrical Engineering, that meant building my own amplifiers during analog-to-digital system upgrades — learning to isolate signal, eliminate interference, and get reliable output from complex systems.

In oil and gas exploration, it meant loading specialized seismic equipment — inserting blasting caps into 80–120 foot shotholes and operating borehole VSI tools with multi-component geophones — to convert mechanical vibrations underground into electrical signals that mapped hydrocarbon reservoirs kilometres below surface. The data had to be right. There was no tolerance for noise.

From there: HVAC systems engineering, where the same precision thinking applied to complex mechanical systems under real-world operating conditions.

Then sales operations — where I discovered that most companies have the same problem as a poorly configured seismic array: too much noise, not enough signal, and nobody quite sure why the output doesn't match expectations.

I fix that. Not with advice — with implementations. Configured CRM systems, automated workflows, live dashboards, documented processes. Systems that generate reliable signal from your sales activity — consistently, repeatably, without the founder in the loop for every decision.

The Philosophy

Three principles that drive every engagement.

These aren't values on a wall. They're engineering constraints I design every system around.

01
Systems problems require systems solutions.
When revenue is underperforming, the first instinct is to blame the people. The real culprit is almost always the process, the tooling, or the data. Fix the system first. The people will follow.
02
Deliverables, not advice.
Anyone can tell you what to do. The value is in doing it — configuring the CRM, building the automation, documenting the playbook. You don't pay for a slide deck. You pay for a running system.
03
Built to outlast the engagement.
Every system I build is documented and designed to run without me. The goal is a sales engine your team owns. A dependency on your consultant is a poorly designed system.
